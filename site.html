Qwerty

Qwerty Is A Keyboard Design For Latin-Script Alphabets.

The Name Comes From The Order Of The First Six Keys On The Top Left Letter Row Of The Keyboard (Q.W.E.R.T.Y).

The Qwerty Design Is Based On A Layout Created For The Sholes And Glidden Typewriter And Sold To E.

Remington And Sons In 1873.

Azerty

Azerty Is A Specific Layout For The Characters Of The Latin Alphabet On Typewriter Keys And Computer Keyboards.

The Layout Takes Its Name From The First Six Letters To Appear On The First Row Of Alphabetical Keys; That Is.

Similar To The German Qwertz Layout, It Is Modelled On The English Qwerty Layout.  

Qwertz

Qwertz Is A Specific Layout For The Characters Of The German Alphabet On Typewriter Keys And Computer Keyboards.

The Layout Takes Its Name From The First Five Letters Of The Qwerty And A The Letter Z (Q.W.E.R.T.Z).

It Is Similar To The Azerty Layout.
